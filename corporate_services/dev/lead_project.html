Marketing

1. Capture the Lead

A Lead is the very first record — a person or company who has shown interest. Create it before any qualification happens.

1
Navigate to Lead

Go to CRM → Lead → New Lead in the ERPNext top menu.

CRM → Lead → New
2
Fill Key Fields

Enter the prospect's Full Name, Company, Email, Phone, and Lead Source (Web, Cold Call, Campaign, etc.).

Required fields
3
Assign & Save

Set Lead Owner (the sales rep responsible) and Status → Open. Save the record.

Status: Open
Tip: Use CRM → Lead Import to bulk-upload leads from a CSV — useful when running a campaign.
Lead Checklist
Marketing → Sales Handoff

2. Qualify & Convert the Lead

Verify the lead has real potential using the BANT framework, then convert it into an Opportunity with one click.

Budget

Does the prospect have the budget to buy your product or service?

Authority

Are you talking to the decision-maker or do you need to escalate?

Need

Does this prospect have a genuine, clear pain point your solution solves?

Timeline

When do they plan to make a purchase or go live?

Qualified — Convert

Open the Lead → click the Convert button at the top. ERPNext auto-creates an Opportunity, a Customer, and a Contact record, then links them all together.

Lead → Convert button
Not Qualified — Mark as Junk/Lost

Set the Lead Status → Do Not Contact or Junk. Enter a reason. The record is kept for future reference but removed from active pipeline views.

Status → Junk / Do Not Contact
Important: Once you click Convert, a Customer record is created automatically. Make sure the company name on the Lead is correct before converting — it becomes the Customer name.

3. Work the Opportunity

The Opportunity tracks the deal through your sales pipeline. Keep it updated as the deal progresses so your pipeline reports are accurate.

1
Set the Sales Stage

Use the Sales Stage field to reflect where you are: Prospecting → Needs Analysis → Proposal → Negotiation → Closure.

Sales Stage field
2
Enter Opportunity Value

Fill Opportunity Amount and Probability %. These drive your weighted pipeline forecasts in the CRM dashboard.

Opportunity Amount + Probability
3
Expected Closing Date

Set Expected Closing Date so pipeline reports show accurate timing. Update this field if the timeline shifts.

4. Create & Send a Quotation

A Quotation is the formal commercial offer you send to the customer for approval.

1
Make Quotation

From the Opportunity screen, click Make → Quotation. Line items, customer, and currency are pre-filled.

Opportunity → Make → Quotation
2
Add Line Items

Add products/services with Qty, Rate, and UOM. Apply item-level or overall discounts as needed.

Items table
3
Set Terms & Taxes

Apply the correct Tax Template and fill in Payment Terms (e.g. 30% advance, 70% on delivery).

Taxes & Charges
4
Submit & Send

Click Submit to lock the Quotation, then use Send Email to share the PDF with the customer.

Submit → Send Email
Customer Accepts

Click Make → Sales Order from the Quotation. Proceed to the next step.

Customer Rejects

Set Quotation status to Lost and fill in the lost reason. Go back to the Opportunity to renegotiate or close as Lost.

Sales

5. Create the Sales Order

The Sales Order is the binding commercial commitment. It triggers inventory reservation, billing, and delivery workflows.

1
Make Sales Order

From the accepted Quotation, click Make → Sales Order. All line items and terms carry over automatically.

Quotation → Make → Sales Order
2
Confirm Delivery Date

Set the Delivery Date per line item. This drives the production and procurement schedules.

Delivery Date (per item)
3
Save & Submit for Approval

Save the SO and route it to your manager for review before submitting. Do not submit until approved.

Save → Route to Manager
Tip: Enable Project in the Sales Order's Items table to track profitability at the item level once the project is created.
Management / Approval

6. Manager Review & Approval

Before the Sales Order becomes legally binding, a manager must review and submit it. Submitting locks all amounts and triggers downstream documents.

1
Review the SO

Check margins, discount %, payment terms, and delivery dates. Compare against the approved pricing policy.

SO Review
2
Approve or Reject

If approved, click Submit. If rejected, leave a comment and send back to Sales for revision using the Assign feature.

Submit / Assign back
3
SO Submitted

Once submitted, the SO is read-only. Inventory is reserved automatically. Billing and delivery documents can now be raised.

Status: Submitted
Approval Checklist
Project Management

7. Create & Run the Project

With the Sales Order submitted, you can now spin up a Project. All timesheets, tasks, and expenses will link back to the SO for profitability tracking.

1
Make Project from SO

Open the submitted Sales Order → click Make → Project. ERPNext creates the Project and links it to the SO automatically.

Sales Order → Make → Project
2
Configure the Project

Set Project Type, Start/End Dates, Project Manager, Estimated Cost, and Billing Method (Fixed Price or T&M).

Project settings
3
Add Tasks & Milestones

Break the project into tasks. Assign Owners, set Due Dates, mark Dependencies, and use the Gantt chart to visualise the schedule.

Project → Tasks tab
4
Log Timesheets & Expenses

Team members log hours via HR → Timesheet → New, selecting the Project and Task. Expenses are recorded under Accounts → Expense Claim linked to the Project.

Timesheet + Expense Claim
5
Track Progress & Close

Update task % Completion regularly. When all tasks are done, set the Project status to Completed. This signals Finance to raise the final invoice.

Status → Completed
Project Checklist
Finance

8. Invoice & Close

The final stage: deliver, invoice, collect payment, and mark everything closed.

1
Delivery Note If goods

For physical goods, create a Delivery Note from the SO to record what was shipped and when.

SO → Make → Delivery Note
2
Sales Invoice

From the SO or Project, click Make → Sales Invoice. Review amounts, submit, and send the invoice PDF to the customer.

SO → Make → Sales Invoice
3
Payment Entry

When payment arrives, create a Payment Entry from the invoice. Match it against the bank statement reconciliation.

Invoice → Make → Payment Entry
4
Close Everything

Mark the SO, Project, and Invoice as Closed/Paid. The Opportunity status auto-updates to Converted.

Status → Closed / Paid
Tip: For T&M projects, use Project → Make → Sales Invoice directly — it pulls timesheet hours and calculates the invoice amount automatically.
